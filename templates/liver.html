Lets Check Out If You have A healthy Liver Or Not

Your liver is your body’s second-largest organ. It sits just under your ribcage on the right side and is about the size of a football. The liver separates nutrients and waste as they move through your digestive system.
It also produces bile, a substance that carries toxins out of your body and aids in digestion.

The term “liver disease” refers to any of several conditions that can affect and damage your liver.
Over time, liver disease can cause cirrhosis (scarring).
As more scar tissue replaces healthy liver tissue, the liver can no longer function properly.
Left untreated, liver disease can lead to liver failure and liver cancer.
